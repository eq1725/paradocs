# Onboarding Overhaul Panel Review — V9.11

**Stimulus:** Chase's brief — first-visit users are asked to share an experience they couldn't explain. That submission becomes their first report and the seed of their profile. After submitting they see their RADAR with similar reports surfaced from the DB, then a brief Lab + site tour.

**Goal:** turn the anonymous browse-first model into an experience-first model where the user is a contributor before they're a consumer.

---

## Why this is interesting

This flips the entire mental model of the app. The current onboarding (when a user lands on `/`) shows them the Today feed and a "Sign up" CTA. They consume content first, then maybe sign up later. The new model says: **before you can browse, tell us what you can't explain.** That:

1. **Generates owned content from minute one.** Every user is a researcher with at least one report.
2. **Personalizes the RADAR before they've done anything else.** The RADAR is empty without seed data; this fills it the moment they sign up.
3. **Increases activation depth.** Users who write something tend to come back at 2-3x the rate of users who only consume (panel reference data).
4. **Creates an emotional hook.** The unexplained experience is personal — the platform "remembers" what they shared.

But it also has real risks the panel will dig into.

---

## The panel

### Mira Chen — Mass-market UX

> The risk with experience-first onboarding is **friction at the wrong moment**. Asking a stranger to type a paranormal experience BEFORE they've seen what the app is for is a big ask. They might bounce. The conversion data on this kind of "share something personal first" pattern is mixed.
>
> The mitigations:
> 1. **Anchor the ask in social proof.** Show them: "Join 50,000 researchers who shared their experience. Here's what others reported this week." Quick visual cue.
> 2. **Lower the bar.** Don't require an essay. A two-sentence description is enough; surface examples ("I saw a craft hover for 3 minutes near my house in 1998") to set the bar low.
> 3. **Optional skip.** Users who explicitly aren't ready should be able to browse without sharing — but the default path is sharing.

**Top recommendation:** lead with examples + social proof. Make the experience field flexible (length-wise). Allow skip with a "browse first, share later" link.

---

### Imani Brooks — Trust & Safety

> Three real risks:
>
> 1. **Bad-faith first reports.** Spam, fake stories for SEO, harassment-vector content. Without moderation, this is the front door for abuse.
> 2. **Private info disclosure.** Users in emotional moments sometimes overshare — exact addresses, real names of others, etc. That stays in the DB forever.
> 3. **CSAM/illegal content via the experience field.** Free-form text can contain anything.

**Top recommendation:** every first-time-submitted experience must go through Claude Haiku moderation (same V9.9 P3 pipeline as bios) BEFORE it's saved. If REJECTED → don't create the profile, don't save the experience, show a generic "couldn't process — please try a different description" error. PENDING → save as draft, queue for admin review, profile is created but the report is hidden until approved.

**Second:** prompt for PII disclosure pattern matching: "Your description mentions what looks like an address — is that intentional?"

---

### Devon Park — Design systems / IA

> The new flow needs three new pages or one beefy multi-step modal:
>
>  - Page 1: experience-share form (the new front door)
>  - Page 2 (or step 2): account creation — email, username (with V9.9 availability), password
>  - Page 3 (or step 3): RADAR reveal + brief tour
>
> Multi-step modal is friendlier than a 3-page redirect chain. Same URL, progress dots, back button works, browser back doesn't lose state. That's the right pattern.
>
> Existing infrastructure to reuse:
> - V9.9 P3 bio moderation → reusable for experience moderation
> - V9.9 P2 username availability check → exact same pattern
> - V9.7 avatar selector → step 4 if we want it
> - ConstellationReveal component (now RADAR) → step 3
> - Existing /api/auth/* infrastructure → step 2

**Top recommendation:** single-page multi-step flow at `/start` (or `/welcome`). Three steps. State held in component-level useState. Submit-all happens at the end — only one network call to create everything.

---

### Rae Kowalski — Retention / growth

> Activation metrics on this kind of flow follow a few well-documented curves:
>
>  - **Step 1 (experience-share)**: 70-80% complete if it's well-designed, 30-40% if it's text-heavy or feels like a homework assignment
>  - **Step 2 (account)**: 80-90% of those who completed step 1 — once they've invested in writing, they sign up
>  - **Step 3 (RADAR reveal)**: 100% — they don't have a choice, but the QUALITY of the reveal determines their D1/D7 retention by 30%+
>
> The single highest-leverage thing: make the RADAR reveal feel magical. They wrote about a UFO sighting; the RADAR shows them 47 OTHER UFO sightings within 100 miles in the last 5 years. That's the moment they convert from "tried it" to "I'm a researcher now."
>
> The lowest-leverage thing: dwelling on the tour. Skip-able. Most users want to play not be lectured.

**Top recommendation:** RADAR reveal is the moment that matters. Invest design budget there. Tour is optional and easy to dismiss.

**Second:** measure D1/D7 retention specifically for users who came through this flow vs the legacy "Sign up to save" CTA. We'll know in 2-3 weeks whether it's working.

---

### Sam Park — Accessibility

> A few hard requirements:
>
> 1. Multi-step flow needs `aria-live="polite"` announcements when step changes
> 2. Each step's heading is the page's main `<h1>` for screen readers
> 3. Form labels visible (no placeholder-only inputs)
> 4. Skip link: "Browse without sharing" should be reachable via keyboard before the form
> 5. Auto-advance on submit (no surprise navigation) — explicit "Continue" button
> 6. Back button preserves all step state

**Top recommendation:** standard multi-step form a11y pattern. Use the same `<AccountNav>`-style sticky chrome that Settings uses for visual continuity once they're inside the app.

---

### Jordan Reyes — Mobile / iOS specialist

> Most paranormal-curious users are coming from mobile. Reddit links, TikTok, Twitter — all mobile-dominant traffic. The flow needs to be mobile-perfect.
>
> Specific requirements:
> 1. Each step fits in a single mobile viewport without scroll where possible
> 2. The experience textarea needs autoresize (no fixed height); inputmode and autocapitalize set
> 3. Email + username have appropriate `inputmode` + `autocomplete` (email, username)
> 4. RADAR reveal on mobile is the harder design — it needs to work on a 375px wide screen, not just 1440px
> 5. Tour is touch-driven (swipe between cards) on mobile, click on desktop

**Top recommendation:** design mobile-first. Validate every step on iPhone 13 PWA before marking done.

---

### Imani Brooks (returning) — Privacy & GDPR

> One legal note: the experience-share form happens BEFORE account creation. We're collecting personal-ish content (their unexplained experience, possibly with location, other-people-references) before the user has agreed to ToS or Privacy Policy.
>
> **Required mitigation:** the experience textarea must be accompanied by a single line: "By sharing, you agree to our [Terms](/terms) and [Privacy Policy](/privacy)." Plus: the experience itself shouldn't auto-publish — it goes to draft state, only published after the account creation step actually completes. If the user bails after writing, no orphaned content.

## Synthesized plan — phased delivery

### Phase 1 — Multi-step `/start` flow (the meat of the work)

1. **New page:** `src/pages/start.tsx` (or `/welcome.tsx`)
2. **Step 1 — Experience share:**
   - Headline: "What did you see?"
   - Subhead: "Researchers join Paradocs by sharing one experience they can't explain. We'll match yours against thousands of other reports."
   - Examples carousel (4-5 short examples to set the bar)
   - Textarea (autoresize, 30-2000 char range)
   - Optional location field (city/region — keep coarse)
   - Optional date/year field
   - "By sharing, you agree to..." line
   - "Skip — browse first" link below the primary CTA
3. **Step 2 — Account creation:**
   - Email (with `inputmode=email autocomplete=email`)
   - Username (V9.9 availability check, real-time)
   - Password (or magic-link option)
   - Display name (optional, defaults to username)
   - Submit creates: profile + first_report (draft state) + signed-in session
4. **Step 3 — RADAR reveal:**
   - Reuses existing `ConstellationReveal` component but renamed contextually
   - Animated transition: "We searched our database…" → reveal of N similar reports
   - Each match is clickable → opens that report
   - Primary CTA: "Open my Lab" → `/lab`
   - Tertiary: "Skip tour, just take me to the feed" → `/discover`
5. **Step 4 (optional, only if user clicks Lab CTA):** mini-tour overlay
   - 3-4 dismissable cards highlighting Lab features
   - Localstorage flag to never show again

### Phase 2 — Backend wiring

- `POST /api/onboarding/submit` — accepts experience text + email/username/password + optional location/date. Creates everything atomically. Runs experience through Claude Haiku moderation. Returns the new auth session + the report id.
- Username availability already exists (V9.9 P2) — reuse directly.
- Bio moderation service (V9.9 P3) — reusable for experience moderation; same pattern.

### Phase 3 — RADAR matching

- New endpoint: `POST /api/onboarding/radar-match?report_id=<id>` — given the new report, find similar reports in the DB. Uses category, keywords, location proximity.
- **Note:** this requires post-mass-ingestion to give meaningful results. Until then, the reveal will show ~1-5 matches at best. Plan for that — don't build a "wow you have 50 matches" UI when the DB has 100 phenomena.

### Phase 4 — Routing changes

- Default unauthenticated `/` route: redirect to `/start` for first-time visitors (localStorage flag for return visitors who skipped)
- Existing "Sign up" CTAs across the site: route to `/start` instead of `/signup`
- Keep `/signup` working for users who explicitly want the legacy form
- Keep `/discover` browseable without auth for the "skip — browse first" path

## What I'd recommend doing first

**Phase 1 + Phase 2 ship first.** The flow works for an arbitrarily small DB — even 1 match makes the RADAR reveal meaningful ("we found one similar report from 1997, near you").

**Phase 3 (RADAR matching) ships separately** — it can launch with a placeholder ("Searching our archive...") that's replaced when matching is live. This decouples onboarding shipping from B1.5 mass-ingestion.

**Phase 4 (routing changes) is the feature flip** — turn it on after Phases 1+2 are tested. Could ship behind a feature flag for gradual rollout.

# REVISIONS — Chase's answers + adjustments (post-panel)

## Answers locked

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Share-first vs anonymous? | **Both options offered in-flow.** Mirror the dashboard pattern — anonymous-or-public toggle, plus profile-public-or-private toggle. User chooses at submission time. |
| Magic link vs password? | **Magic link primary** for mass-scale mobile-first apps. See "Auth choice rationale" below. |
| First-report privacy default? | **RADAR-matching-only.** User can change in the form (toggle to fully public OR fully private). |
| Experience scope? | **All schema-fitting experiences.** UFO sightings, vivid dreams, déjà vu, alien abductions, sleep paralysis, NDEs, OBEs, drug-induced experiences, ghosts, cryptids, etc. Broad spectrum — anything the platform's broader schema accepts. |
| Mobile vs desktop? | **Mobile first.** Validated on iPhone 13 PWA before desktop polish. |

## Auth choice rationale: magic link primary

For mass-scale + mobile-first + paranormal-curious demographic:

1. **Friction:** magic link cuts the password-creation step entirely. On mobile, password entry is the #1 abandonment point in onboarding (~15-25% drop-off).
2. **Security:** no password stored = no password breach surface. Stripe-grade compliance auto-met.
3. **Lost-account recovery:** magic link IS recovery. No "forgot password" loop.
4. **Industry pattern:** Substack, Notion, Linear, Slack, Vercel, Loom all magic-link first. Optional password as a later upgrade for power users.
5. **Trade-off:** email round-trip adds 30-90 seconds. Mitigated by (a) showing a clear "Check your email" screen with the email address visible, (b) keeping the user signed in once authenticated for ~14 days.

**Decision:** Phase 1 ships magic-link only. Password optional comes later if power users ask. Supabase Auth supports both natively.

## Plan adjustments

### Adjustment 1 — Skip option must be prominent

Panel called this out lightly; Chase wants it **required and visible**. Skip should be:
- **Visible from step 1**, not buried in a corner
- Labeled positively: "Browse Paradocs first" (not "Skip" — sounds like dismissal)
- Tertiary visual weight (text link, not button)
- Location: directly below the primary CTA in step 1
- Behavior: sets a localStorage flag `paradocs_onboarding_skipped: true` so we don't re-prompt next visit

If skipped, the user lands on `/discover` and the global header CTA stays "Sign up" (legacy). They can return to `/start` anytime to share their experience.

### Adjustment 2 — Report submission balance

Don't force depth, but **make depth easy and rewarding** for users who want it. Two-tier approach:

**Tier 1 (Quick) — single screen, ~30 seconds:**
- Experience textarea (autoresize, 30-2000 chars)
- Quick category guess via Claude (we send the text, get back UFO/Cryptid/Ghost/etc. as a default — user can override)
- Optional location field (city/region only — keep coarse)
- Optional date/year field
- Visibility toggle (RADAR-matching-only / public / private)
- **Examples carousel** below the textarea: real reports from our DB, dynamically loaded. Shows that simple is fine.
- **"Add more details"** chevron expands to Tier 2.

**Tier 2 (Deep) — collapsed by default, opens on demand:**
- Witness count
- Time of day / weather conditions / lighting
- Photo/video upload (reuses existing Cloudinary pipeline + V9.7 Rekognition)
- Related conditions (sleep state for dreams; substance use for drug-induced; etc.)
- Aftereffects
- Other people who shared the experience

This way, users who write 50 chars submit a clean basic report. Users who fill all of Tier 2 get a feature-rich report that helps RADAR matching dramatically.

**Future task:** broader `/submit` page overhaul to match this two-tier pattern. Currently it's a fixed multi-step wizard.

### Adjustment 3 — Examples are real reports, not testimonials

Pull 4-5 real reports from the DB at page-load time. Filter by:
- ≥30 chars, ≤200 chars (keep examples short)
- Approved status only
- Diverse categories (rotate UFO/Ghost/Cryptid/NDE/OBE/Sleep paralysis)

Render as a fade-rotating carousel below the textarea. Refreshes on each visit.

### Adjustment 4 — Tighten moderation, especially for first reports

User experiences in onboarding are HIGHER VALUE than typical content. False positives are catastrophic — we don't want to deny someone's UFO sighting because Claude flagged "abduction" as borderline.

**Specific tightenings:**

1. **Custom system prompt for first-report moderation** (separate from bio moderation):
   - Explicit: paranormal content (UFO, abduction, alien, ghost, demon, possession, NDE, OBE, sleep paralysis, drugs/substances when in NDE/OBE/dream context, religious/spiritual experiences, cryptids) is **APPROVED by default**
   - Hard reject only on: hate speech / slurs targeting protected classes, doxxing of named real people, threats of violence, CSAM, scam/spam pitches
   - Pending only on: obvious PII oversharing (full address + phone), graphic gore (not paranormal-context)
   - Default decision when ambiguous: **APPROVED** (we'd rather review later than deny upfront)
2. **Higher hard-reject threshold:** require Claude's confidence to be very high before reject. If unsure → approve.
3. **Skip moderation for empty / obviously-test inputs:** don't burn tokens on "asdf" etc.

This costs us slightly more admin queue review work, but protects the experience-share funnel from false-positive friction.

### Adjustment 5 — Use the LIVE RADAR feature, not legacy ConstellationReveal

The existing `LabConstellationTab` is the canonical RADAR. The onboarding reveal step should use the **same data layer** (matching API, similarity scoring) to render the same RADAR view, just framed as a first-look reveal. **Don't fork.**

Specifically:
- Reuse `/api/constellation/match` (or whatever the Lab uses) for matching
- Reuse the visualization component or a stripped-down version of it
- "Open Lab" CTA in the reveal step takes them to `/lab` where they see the FULL RADAR with their matches loaded

When the separate "RADAR look + function" session ships improvements, the onboarding reveal automatically inherits them.

### Adjustment 6 — Anonymous-or-public toggle in the experience-share form

Match the dashboard pattern. Two independent toggles:

**Toggle 1 — This report's visibility:**
- 🔒 **Private** (only you and our research team can see this)
- 🔍 **RADAR matches only** (default — visible only when other users have similar experiences)
- 🌍 **Public** (anyone can read this report)

**Toggle 2 — Share as:**
- 👤 **Yourself** (your @username + display name attached)
- 👻 **Anonymous** (no identity attached; RADAR matching still works, but shown as "Researcher")

These mirror the existing Privacy panel toggle in /account/settings, which sets `constellation_public`.
