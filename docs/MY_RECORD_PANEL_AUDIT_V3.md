# My Record V3 — Match Views + Cohort Rooms

**To:** Chase
**From:** The V3 Panel (seven voices, convened June 2026)
**Re:** The founder's reframe — show users which catalogued reports match theirs and how; replace 1:1 named matches with earned-access cohort rooms.
**Panel:** Peer-support / group-therapy designer (PG); Investigative-research PM (IR); Subculture community designer (SC); Cultural anthropologist of paranormal community (CA); Editorial / documentary lead (ED); Conversion + retention (CR); Mass-market product designer (MM).

---

## 1. TL;DR

The flagship of My Record is the **Resonance Map** — a single utilitarian surface that shows the user, for each of their submitted reports, the catalogued reports it matches and the specific signals on which it matches. Not "your astrology;" a working concordance. The flagship social mechanic is the **Casebook** — an auto-formed cohort room of 6-12 contributors whose reports share a tight, corpus-rare signal-set, gated by submission (you got in because you logged a comparable account), centred on a single shared object the group maintains over time: an annotated **Case File** that aggregates their reports, the corpus's seven prior accounts, a running notes column, and a weekly editorial prompt from Paradocs. Tier ladder simplifies: Free reads the Map and previews Casebooks; Basic ($5.99) earns Casebook seats and posts inside them; Pro ($14.99) keeps the Dossier + Watchlists + Tracer mechanic. The Casebook differentiates against r/UFOs because everyone in the room has filed a real, structurally comparable report against a 200k corpus — Reddit can't gate on that. Editorial vetoed two strong concepts during synthesis: a "Hall of Echoes" gallery view of strangers' reports (twee gallery register) and a "Witness Convergence Score" composite number (replaces a transparent multi-axis match with a stat). Four dissents recorded, including a hard CR dissent on whether Casebooks form fast enough on n=1 submissions to be a Basic-tier flagship.

## 2. The recommended My Record — what a user sees

### Mobile, top-to-bottom

1. **Header strip.** "My Record" wordmark · Submit pill · settings cog. No notification bell — alerts live on the Casebook row.
2. **Submitted reports list.** A compact stack of every account the user has logged: date · location · phen-family label · one-line excerpt. Each row tappable into a full report view; kebab for edit/archive. This is the user's own record, not the catalogue's view of them. Lives at the top because it is the *user's*.
3. **Resonance Map.** Per-report, a structured concordance view (see §3). The dominant surface. ~3 viewport-heights on mobile.
4. **Your Casebooks.** A 1-3 row strip showing the cohort rooms the user has earned a seat in. Each row: cohort name (catalogue-derived, plain — e.g., "1990s NC triangle, ridgeline, no-shadow"), member count, "3 new in the Case File since you were last here," tap to enter. Free users see *eligible-but-locked* Casebooks below the active ones, with a "Subscribe to take your seat" CTA on each.
5. **Pro surfaces** (Pro users only) — Dossier export pill, Watchlists rail, Tracer status. Folded compact.

### Desktop

Two-column above lg: left column is the Resonance Map for the active report (selector at top of column to switch reports). Right column 360px sticky: Submitted-reports list (compact), then Your Casebooks. Pro surfaces below the left column. The single most-used affordance — switching between your own reports — never requires a scroll.

Mobile target scroll: ~2,800px on n=1 Free. The v1 page was 6,400. The Map and the Casebook strip absorb everything the v1 page split across 12 sections.

### 3.2 What corpus signals it surfaces

- **Phenomenology lineage.** The 1-3 catalogue phen-pages the user's account inherits from, with the sibling families and counts. Sourced from the existing classifier output.
- **Closest 5 reports + "View all N".** Pulled from the existing constellation RPC; signal-overlap badge per row uses the locked 8-signal weights from `named-match/fingerprint.ts` (phen_family 0.30, subfamily 0.25, descriptors 0.15, geo 0.13, decade 0.07, time-of-day 0.05, multi-witness 0.025, media 0.025). The full list lives on `/lab/related/[reportId]`.
- **Per-signal match bars.** The eight signals as horizontal bars, where the bar length is how many of the 47 share that signal. This is the substance — the user can see at a glance which dimensions are dense (decade, phen-family) and which are thin (media, multi-witness). Tap a row → the 47-report list filters to only that signal.
- **Rarity reading, plain.** A short prose paragraph computing the user's position inside the sub-corpus on the signal-set (the existing Pro Dossier rarity computation, surfaced at all tiers). Names *which signals* contribute the rarity, not a single percentile. The link goes to the 47 thinnest comparable accounts.
- **Witness adjacency.** Promoted out of the v1 MatchList. When ≥3 corpus reports describe the same event-shape (high signal overlap, geographically and temporally close), surface the line. It is the strongest emotional payoff on the page.

### 3.3 What's actionable

Every line is a destination. Tap a phen-family chip → open the family page. Tap a closest-report row → open the full report. Tap a signal bar → filter the related-accounts list. Tap "see the 47 thinnest" → the rarity sub-corpus list. Tap the witness-adjacency line → opens the user's eligible Casebook (the bridge from Map to Cohort Room).

### 3.4 What's clickable that pulls the user deeper into the catalogue

The Resonance Map is the front door to the rest of Paradocs. Each row pulls the user into a corpus surface — family page, related-accounts list, sub-corpus rarity list, Casebook room — not back into their own loop. **The Map is a tool for reading the catalogue against yourself.** This is what differentiates Paradocs from a vibes-render: every line answers a "what did you find?" question with a citeable corpus link.

### 3.5 Reference patterns

The closest external pattern is the **Bellingcat lead-board** crossed with the **NUFORC monthly digest** — the user's contribution is shown against an ongoing investigation's structured findings. The match-bar grid borrows the **23andMe relative-finder** convention (multiple axes, one composite per axis, transparently shown). The signal weighting is **MUFON case-file** language. The Map is not a single-number rarity score (CR explicitly killed that — see §8); it is a multi-axis read.

### 3.6 Editorial stamp (ED)

**ON-BRAND.** "The Map is a concordance — a research instrument. The visual register is closer to a library card catalogue than to a dashboard. Per-signal bars are tools, not decorations. The one thing I will police: the prose lines ('the thinness comes from…') must be deterministic-templated from the actual signal contributions, not generative. The moment we let Sonnet write that line we get spook-tour-guide voice. Template it from the underlying numbers."

### 3.7 Match-view candidates that lost

The panel considered six concepts before landing on the Map:

- **A. Concordance grid (chosen).** What you read above. Won on substance + utility.
- **B. Hall of Echoes.** A horizontally-scrollable gallery of strangers' accounts that "resonate" with yours, each as a card with a pull-quote. **ED veto.** Twee — the gallery register turns the catalogue into a museum-of-strangeness, which is the brand cliché we are trying to avoid. The user does not benefit from reading 47 strangers' pull-quotes in series.
- **C. Witness Convergence Score.** A single 0-100 "how corroborated is your account" number with a sub-corpus position. **CR veto.** Collapses the multi-axis match into one number, which is what people complain about in 23andMe's ancestry composites. The Map's per-signal bars give the same answer transparently.
- **D. Time-Machine context strip.** What else the catalogue captured the same week within the region. **Folded into Map** as a small inline card; not flagship on its own. (This was already proposed in the Pro Dossier §3.1.8 as a section; the V3 panel kept it there and pointed to it from the Map.)
- **E. Match Confidence Ladder.** A vertical ladder of reports from "definitely related" to "loosely related" with thresholds named editorially. **Held for V2.** Useful but redundant with the ranked 47.
- **F. Corpus Lineage Tree.** A literal tree visualization rooting the user's report into the phen-family taxonomy with sibling nodes. **MM veto.** Tree visualizations are read-difficult on mobile, and the Phenomenology Lineage card carries the same information in 4 lines of prose. Cut.

**Flagship: A (Map). Supporting: D (Time-Machine strip, embedded).**

## 4. The Casebook — the cohort room

### 4.1 The mechanic in one sentence

A Casebook is a 6-12-person cohort room, formed automatically when enough contributors' reports share a tight, corpus-rare signal-set, and centred on a single shared object the cohort builds together: an annotated Case File aggregating their accounts, the corpus's prior accounts of the same shape, and a weekly editorial prompt from Paradocs.

### 4.2 How cohorts are formed

The cohort detector runs nightly and operates on the same signal vocabulary the Map uses. The rule (PG + IR + SC settled on this after debate):

- **Required minimum.** Phen-family + subfamily match. Without both, no cohort consideration. (This is the same hard floor `named-match/fingerprint.ts` already enforces for confidence ≥0.60.)
- **Differentiating signals.** ≥3 of the next six signals (descriptors, geo, decade, time-of-day, multi-witness, media) match across the cohort candidates. The match is *across the set*, not pairwise — a cohort exists when 6+ reports cluster on the same 5-signal subset.
- **Corpus rarity gate.** The clustered signal-subset must appear in ≤2% of the relevant sub-corpus. This is what makes the cohort meaningful — being in the room means your account sits in the long tail, not the modal cluster.
- **Optional cohort hashtag.** Sub-corpus topical descriptor extracted at cohort birth (e.g., "no-shadow," "static-tingling," "missing-time") used as the cohort's name. Plain phrase, not branded.

A cohort's birth is a discrete event. The cohort gets a stable URL (`/lab/casebook/[id]`), a name, and a member set. The first 6 members are seeded from the catalogue (existing high-signal-overlap reports whose contributors are Basic+ and opted-in to discoverability). Subsequent users join when their submitted report scores into the cluster.

### 4.3 Group size

**Target: 6-12 members. Hard cap: 20.** The literature (PG cites IANDS small-groups; the John Mack abductee groups; Wisdo cohort studies; AA meeting size) is unanimous that intimate-disclosure groups break above ~15. SC adds that Discord servers under 50 routinely outperform >500 servers on per-member contribution; small is the differentiator.

When a cohort hits 20 active members, the system *forks*: the matcher proposes a sub-cluster (typically the rarer half on a thinner signal-subset), and the forked cohort gets its own Case File. Original cohort is archived as "graduated" — read-only for original members.

### 4.4 Earned access — the gating rule

**You see a Casebook seat offer when your submitted report scores into a cohort's cluster.** The seat is not transferable, not purchasable, not gameable. Free users see the seat as locked with a Basic CTA ("Take your seat — $5.99/mo"). Basic users accept the seat and enter. Pro users get one additional ability: they can propose a guest expert (see §5.7).

This is the load-bearing differentiation. **You did not pay your way in. You did not friend your way in. You filed an account, and the catalogue placed you.** Compare to:

- **r/UFOs.** Anyone can post. Quality flood — content is unverified, undated, ungeolocated, untagged. Joining a thread requires nothing.
- **Facebook groups.** Anyone the admin lets in. Vibes-moderated. Most posts are media-shares, not first-person accounts.
- **Discord servers.** Invite-based, then open. No structural gating on the user's actual experience.
- **NUFORC online forums.** Open. No structural gating; the database is read-only.
- **MUFON CMS.** Investigator-only; the cohort access is professional, not experiential.

**The Casebook is the only product where you and the five other people in the room are there because you each filed a structurally comparable, corpus-rare account.** That is the moat.

**The Case File is the room's spine.** It is structured, editable in §4 (Running notes), and refreshed weekly with new prompts from the Paradocs editorial generator (PG: "this is the AA-meeting-agenda equivalent — the room has a reason to convene this week, not just a default of 'chat'"). Chat is a side column, not the centerpiece. PG: "If the chat is the centerpiece, you've built Discord. If the document is the centerpiece, you've built a working group."

### 4.6 Activity model — async, scheduled, real-time

- **Async by default.** All actions land in the Case File and are visible on the next visit. Notes are durable; the chat column persists but is not the artifact.
- **One scheduled event per week.** Every Monday at 09:00 local-cohort-time (calculated from cohort members' inferred timezones — fallback ET), the Paradocs editorial cron fires a new prompt into §This week's prompt. The prompt is generated against the cohort's signal-set: newly-catalogued matching reports, a historical parallel, a question the catalogue raised. (See §5 for the prompt library.)
- **Real-time when present.** Lightweight presence dots show when a member is in the room; an inline "X is typing" appears in the chat column. No push for chat. No "active now" surfacing outside the room — privacy floor.
- **Notification budget.** One push or email per member per week, batched. Carries: new prompt + N new notes since last visit + N new members. This is the existing watchlist-digest cadence (`PRO_TIER_VALIDATION_V3` §6) extended to cohorts.

### 4.7 Moderation

Tiered (PG settled this):

- **Self-moderation primary.** Members can flag a note or message. 3 flags hide it pending editorial review. Cohort norms set in a 5-line "house rules" pinned to the Case File header at cohort birth (Paradocs writes them; cohort can amend by 2/3 vote — see §5.3).
- **Editorial team secondary.** Reviews flagged content within 48 hours. Hard kill on doxxing, threats, off-topic recruiting.
- **Trust system tertiary.** A member with ≥3 substantive notes in a cohort earns "Contributor" mark next to their name. Two Contributors can co-sign a "move on" vote that retires a flagged thread without editorial review. This is a workload safety valve.
- **No external moderators.** No volunteer mod army. The room is small enough that the cohort can self-tend.

ED stamp: **ON-BRAND with caveat.** "House rules must read as a museum's reading-room rules — quiet, non-twee, no behavioural cliché. I'll draft them."

### 4.8 The persistent artifact

The Case File itself is the artifact. It accumulates over the cohort's life. Pro members can export a one-time PDF snapshot of their cohort's Case File (per-member export, redacted of others' verbatim text unless those members opt in to inclusion — same anonymization controls as the Pro Dossier share).

Long-term, when a cohort graduates (archived), the Case File becomes a **public Catalogue Annex** entry — anonymized, signal-set named, with the editorial-curated finding ("Eight contributors describing 1990s NC ridgeline triangles converged on the no-shadow descriptor; the 1988 archival photo corroborates."). This feeds the catalogue back. CA: "This is the *ethnographic loop* — the group's collaborative reading goes back into the corpus as a finding. That is the IANDS / John Mack group output pattern made operational."

### 4.9 Dissolution / silence rules

- **Silence threshold.** If a cohort has zero notes and zero chat for 60 days, it enters dormant state — Casebook row dimmed, no further prompts, members can still post to reawaken.
- **Dormant > 180 days.** Cohort archives. Members notified one week prior. They retain read access in perpetuity; Case File becomes static.
- **Merge rule.** A dormant cohort whose signal-set sits within 1 signal of an active cohort's signal-set is proposed for merge. Both sides' active members vote (simple majority of those who vote within 7 days). Merge is the editorial-team-executed event; default no-merge.
- **Fork rule** (§4.3) is the inverse: too-large cohort splits.

### 4.10 Cohort-mechanic candidates that lost

- **A. Casebook (chosen).** Cohort of 6-12, Case File centerpiece, weekly prompt, async. Won on substance.
- **B. Field-Team mode.** A standing 6-person team that hunts for new matching reports together — gamified contribution. **PG veto.** "Gamification of paranormal experience-reporting is brand suicide. People filed these accounts because they're trying to make sense of something real."
- **C. Weekly Convening.** A scheduled 1-hour live text chat once a week, with a moderator. **MM veto.** "Scheduled-live for an English-speaking-mainstream-mass-market product is a 5% feature. Async is the only way this scales."
- **D. The Pair.** Cohort-of-two — match a user to one other person with the strongest signal overlap, indefinitely. **CA veto.** "This is the v1 named-match mechanic rebranded. Founder rejected. Two people without a third entity in the room is a parasocial relationship; three+ with a shared object is a working group."
- **E. The Roundtable.** Open-membership cohorts (anyone with one matching submission can join). **SC veto.** "Open membership reverts the gating that is our entire moat. The point is that the room is small and earned."

**Flagship: A (Casebook). Supporting: a Pair-mode (D, narrowed) for the long-tail singular-rarity case where a true cohort cannot form — see §11.**

### 4.11 Why this is genuinely valuable, in one sentence

A Casebook lets six to twelve people whose accounts share a corpus-rare event-shape collaboratively read the catalogue against their shared experience and produce a finding the individual could not.

## 5. What the group does together — group activities

The cohort's recurring activity is the test of whether the mechanic earns its weight. Six activities, in order of priority:

### 5.1 Annotate the weekly prompt

Each Monday, the editorial generator fires a prompt into §This week's prompt. Three prompt types rotate:

- **"New matches landed."** N newly-catalogued reports matching the cohort's signal-set. The cohort reads them, votes whether each one belongs in the cohort (2/3 majority adds the contributor as a candidate seat), and adds notes to the Case File.
- **"Historical parallel."** An older corpus report (pre-1990, or from another continent) that shares the cohort's signal-set. The cohort reads it and discusses whether the parallel is real or surface.
- **"The catalogue's question."** A question the corpus raised that the cohort can answer — e.g., "Five of you describe 02-04h; three describe 22-00h. Is the late-evening cluster a separate phenomenon or a perceptual variation?"

User motivation: small, recurring, low-stakes — show up Monday, read one thing, leave one note. Editorial benefit: notes become source material for the catalogue's evolving descriptor vocabulary (CA: "the IANDS researcher-loop"). Engagement payoff: gives the user a reason to return next Monday.

### 5.2 Co-author the Case File §Running notes

The §Running notes block is the cohort's collaborative summary of their finding. Edit history visible per-line; line-edit conflicts resolved by 2/3 vote. The §Running notes is the artifact that, on cohort graduation, becomes the public Annex entry. PG: "This is the AA-meeting-minutes / Wisdo group-summary pattern. The group's accumulated text *is* the value."

### 5.3 Vote on house rules + amendments

At cohort birth, Paradocs seeds 5 house rules. The cohort can amend any rule by 2/3 vote (per-member, decay after 14 days of no participation). This is the *governance* affordance — small, low-frequency, but real. CA: "Subculture groups die without governance; they fracture or stagnate. A genuine amend mechanism is the difference between Wikipedia (alive) and Facebook groups (dead)."

### 5.4 Adopt a new submission

When a non-member's new submission scores into the cohort's signal-set, the cohort sees it as a candidate. 2/3 vote to extend a seat offer. The new member joins. This is the **earned-by-the-catalogue-then-confirmed-by-peers** double gate. The cohort owns its boundary.

### 5.5 Catalogue-question answering

Periodic editorial prompts ask the cohort a research question — "of the 7 prior corpus accounts and your 8, how many describe the silent-craft detail? How many specifically mention no-shadow under the craft?" The cohort's vote tally becomes a data point in the catalogue's descriptor refinement. IR: "This is Zooniverse-style microtask, but with stakes — these are the people who can actually answer because they lived the event-shape."

### 5.6 Mark dispute and convergence

The Case File surfaces, automatically, where the cohort agrees and disagrees. E.g., "8 of 8 cite triangle. 7 of 8 cite silent. 5 of 8 cite no-shadow. 3 of 8 cite ozone smell." The dispute and convergence map *is* the finding. PG: "This is the consensus-board pattern from group therapy; making the agreement visible is more important than reaching it."

### 5.7 Pro-member: propose a guest

A Pro member can propose, via the Casebook actions menu, that the editorial team invite a guest — a researcher (Jacques Vallée-school), a journalist, an archivist — for a one-week scheduled AMA inside the room. Paradocs editorial vets and reaches out. Approximately 5-10 guest weeks per quarter across all cohorts. This is the **rare aspirational** affordance that justifies Pro on the social side. CA: "Mack ran small groups with rotating researcher presence. The model exists."

## 6. Differentiation vs. Reddit / Facebook / Discord — explicit

| Dimension | r/UFOs | FB UFO groups | Discord servers | **Paradocs Casebook** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Who's in the room | Anyone | Whoever admin lets in | Whoever has invite | **Only contributors whose filed account matched the cohort's signal-set** |
| Quality of accounts | Mostly media-share, low first-person | Mostly media-share | Mostly off-topic | **100% first-person, dated, geocoded, classified, tagged** |
| Room size | 3M | 5k-500k | 100-50k | **6-20, hard-capped** |
| Recurring activity | New posts in feed | Endless scroll | Always-on chat | **Weekly editorial prompt + Case File** |
| Persistent artifact | None (posts disappear) | None | None | **Case File grows over cohort's life** |
| Content authority | Random | Admin's taste | Mod's taste | **Editorial team + cohort vote** |
| Connection to a corpus | None | None | None | **200k report archive in the room as a member** |
| What you leave with | Nothing | Nothing | Maybe a friend | **A finding co-authored with 7 peers** |
| Why a user picks this | Volume | Familiarity | Friends | **Because the people in the room had your experience and the catalogue says so** |

The answer to "I could just post this on r/UFOs" is: **you'd post it to 3M anonymous lurkers, get 4 comments, and learn nothing. In a Casebook, you'd land in a room of 8 people who filed structurally comparable reports and a Case File showing what the 200k archive thinks of your shared event-shape.**

## 8. Tier value redistribution

### Free — "you can read the map"

- Full Resonance Map on every submitted report.
- See your eligible Casebooks (locked, with member count and "X new in the Case File" preview).
- Read the public Catalogue Annexes (graduated cohorts' findings).
- Submit unlimited reports.
- Receive Map updates when the corpus grows.

### Basic — $5.99 — "you take your seat"

- Everything Free.
- **Accept Casebook seats when offered.** Post to Case File, vote, co-author Running notes.
- Receive the weekly prompt notification (one per cohort, batched).
- Discoverability toggle (your submissions are eligible to seat you in cohorts).
- View members' display names (first name + last initial; pseudonyms also permitted).
- Receive the Map's per-signal data depth (configurable filter sets).

### Pro — $14.99 — "you steward + export"

- Everything Basic.
- **The Dossier** (per the V3 spec — kept whole). 7-section structured cross-reference per submitted report, PDF export, share card.
- **Custom Watchlists** (per the V3 spec — kept whole).
- **Tracer mechanic.** Leave-a-trace on a signal in your Resonance Map; future submissions in that signal-set fire a Tracer alert into your Casebook (if any) or a personal notice.
- **Casebook PDF export.** One-tap export of your cohort's Case File as a snapshot PDF.
- **Propose-a-guest** (one per quarter per cohort).
- **Rare-Crossing alert.** When your submission seats you into a cohort whose signal-set sits in the thinnest 1% of the corpus, you get same-day notification (Basic gets it on next weekly batch).

**Argument for this distribution (CR + MM):**

The Casebook is the engagement loop. Putting it at Basic is the right call because: (a) it makes the $5.99 a clear ask — "earn your seat in a working room of peers"; (b) it does not over-stuff Free, which would reduce conversion pressure; (c) the social mechanic is what most users will *talk about*, and Basic is where that word-of-mouth needs to land. The Pro flagship stays the Dossier + Watchlists (already validated, per V3 Pro spec), and the cohort-side Pro perks (export, guest, rare-alerts) are *stewardship affordances*, not access affordances.

CR dissent (recorded in §11): Free→Basic conversion requires a Casebook to exist for the user. For users with very common phen-families and no rare signals, no cohort may form in the first 30 days. The Map can sustain Free engagement; the Basic ask depends on cohort supply.

## 11. Dissents and open questions for the founder

### 11.1 CR — Cohort supply on n=1 commons

"Casebooks at Basic only convert if a cohort exists for the user. A new user filing a 'standard' triangle UFO sighting in a populated state may not score into a rare-enough cluster to seat them. If we hold the rarity gate at ≤2% sub-corpus prevalence, ~40-60% of new submissions will find no cohort offer in the first 30 days. That's a Basic conversion hole.

**Two mitigations:** (a) loosen the rarity gate to ≤5% prevalence, accepting larger and less-tight cohorts (worse experience, more supply); (b) ship a 'Pair mode' for singular-rarity users — when no cohort forms, the user is offered a one-other-person reading-only pair (no chat) on the single closest matching account, with the Case File reduced to the two accounts + the corpus's prior matches.

**Panel split 4-3 on which to ship first.** I (CR) want (a) for Basic conversion. PG and CA want (b) for experience integrity. Founder call."

### 11.2 PG — The 60/180-day silence rule

"Silence-to-archive in 180 days is the literature standard for group therapy, but for a corpus-anchored product, a silent cohort is not the same as a dead one — the catalogue may grow into the cohort's signal-set six months later. Proposal: keep dormant state visible to members indefinitely; only archive when *both* (a) no activity 180+ days *and* (b) no new corpus matches for the signal-set in 90+ days. Stricter on the catalogue-side trigger."

### 11.3 CA — The cohort's right to its own findings

"When a Case File §Running notes becomes a public Catalogue Annex on graduation, the cohort's collaborative authorship is being absorbed into Paradocs's corpus. The cohort should have the right to (a) opt out of the Annex; (b) co-byline the Annex; (c) edit the Annex post-graduation. This is the IRB-style researcher-versus-participant question.

**Recommend:** default opt-in to Annex; cohort vote (2/3) to opt out; co-byline by cohort name not individuals (the cohort name is corpus-derived, not personal). Founder needs to make a call on the editorial-rights question before any cohort graduates."

### 11.4 SC — Anti-recruitment policy

"Subculture communities die fastest from external recruitment — a Casebook member starts posting cohort links to r/UFOs or a Telegram channel, inviting outsiders to 'check this out.' The product's structural gate prevents joining without a matching submission, but linking to the cohort URL still creates an audience expectation that breaks the room.

**Recommend:** Casebook URLs are member-only (auth-gated). No public preview of an active cohort's Case File; only graduated Annexes are public. Members caught soliciting external attention are subject to cohort-vote removal (2/3, with editorial review)."

### 11.5 Open questions for the founder

1. **Cohort birth threshold.** What's the minimum seeded membership before a cohort goes live? Panel default: 6 from existing corpus (highest-signal matches, Basic+ opt-in). Could go as low as 4 if we need faster cohort formation early in the product's life.
2. **First-cohort guarantee on Basic upgrade.** Should we promise every new Basic subscriber a cohort seat within 14 days? Panel split — would require some hand-curation in the early period. Likely yes for the first 100 Basic subscribers.
3. **Cohort hashtag editorial review.** Auto-generated cohort names (e.g., "1990s NC triangle, ridgeline, no-shadow") need an editorial QA pass before going live. Who owns this — founder, ED panelist, an operator?
4. **Pro guest workflow.** Who reaches out to the proposed guest? Editorial team capacity in the first quarter probably caps at 4 guest weeks total. Set Pro expectations accordingly.
5. **Cohort-of-one for the truly singular.** When a user's signal-set is so thin that the corpus contains no other matching accounts, a cohort cannot form. Proposal: the user is offered a "watching seat" — they receive a personal alert whenever a matching submission lands, indefinitely, and a cohort proposal fires the moment 3 matches exist. Free or Pro?
6. **Multi-cohort membership.** A user with three reports may earn seats in three cohorts. Cap (one Casebook per user) or no cap? Panel default: no cap, but the My Record strip displays at most 3 active cohort rows with a "view all" link.
