# Pricing Hero Image Candidates — `/pricing` Page

## Brief

The Paradocs `/pricing` page should read as a **museum-membership page**, not a SaaS marketing screenshot. The hero image needs to evoke the vintage-archival mood of a national archive or library of human experience — paranormal-adjacent but tasteful, atmospheric, and contemplative (no ghosts, no UFOs, no Halloween). The brand color `#9000F0` will be overlaid as a tint/gradient, so the image must be relatively dark and tonally cohesive. Format: landscape, 2000px+ wide, public-domain or CC-licensed only.

**Overall recommendation:** **Option 1 (Trouvelot Aurora Borealis, 1872).** It is the strongest brand fit — vintage-archival without being kitsch, depicts a real and well-attested atmospheric phenomenon (rather than anything paranormal-coded), and its dark indigo/black sky with vertical violet ribbons of aurora reads beautifully as the substrate for a `#9000F0` overlay. The chromolithograph register also signals "archive" and "catalogue" in a way that a modern photograph cannot. Option 2 is the strong second pick if the panel wants a more contemporary, photographic register; Option 3 is the dark-horse pick if they want to lean fully into the "documentary chart" / "encyclopedic" identity.

## Option 1 — Trouvelot, *Aurora Borealis* (1872)

- **Title:** Étienne Léopold Trouvelot, "Aurora Borealis: As Observed March 1, 1872, at 9h. 25m. P.M." — Plate IV in *The Trouvelot Astronomical Drawings: Atlas* (Charles Scribner's Sons, 1882).
- **Direct image URL:** `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/60/Trouvelot_-_Aurora_Borealis_-_1872.jpg`
- **Source page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Trouvelot_-_Aurora_Borealis_-_1872.jpg
- **License:** Public Domain (author died 1895; PD-old-100). Source scan from New York Public Library released as **CC0**. No attribution required, but credit "Étienne Léopold Trouvelot / NYPL / Wikimedia Commons" is courteous.
- **Dimensions:** 9,315 × 7,301 px (12.94 MB, JPEG). Aspect ratio ~1.28:1 — broad landscape, well-suited to a 16:9 hero crop with sky bleeding edge-to-edge.
- **Why it works:**
  1. Trouvelot's chromolithographs are the canonical pre-photographic record of celestial phenomena — they read instantly as "scientific archive."
  2. The composition is a deep indigo-to-violet vertical-banded sky with a low silhouetted horizon — already in the brand color family. Overlay with `#9000F0` will deepen rather than fight it.
  3. Subject (aurora borealis) is a real atmospheric phenomenon historically conflated with paranormal sightings, which threads the editorial needle perfectly — wondrous without being woo.
- **Treatment recommendation:** Crop to 16:9 from the upper two-thirds (sky-dominant). Apply a `#9000F0` gradient at 45–55% opacity from bottom-left → top-right; leave the top-right ~30% of the frame at ~20% tint so headline text sits cleanly on the darkest part of the aurora ribbons. Consider a subtle vignette to drive eye toward the CTA stack.

## Option 2 — NASA / Josh Cassada, Aurora over Manitoba (ISS068-E-59877, Feb 28, 2023)

- **Title:** "A Dazzling Aurora Borealis" — astronaut photograph ISS068-E-59877, taken by NASA astronaut Josh Cassada from the International Space Station as it passed over southeastern Manitoba during the G3 geomagnetic storm of late February 2023.
- **Direct image URL:** `https://assets.science.nasa.gov/content/dam/science/esd/eo/images/imagerecords/151000/151043/ISS068-E-59877_lrg.jpg`
- **Source page:** https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/a-dazzling-aurora-borealis-151043/ (originally https://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/images/151043/a-dazzling-aurora-borealis)
- **License:** Public Domain (NASA imagery is not subject to copyright in the United States; attribution "NASA Earth Observatory / Josh Cassada / Expedition 68" is requested as a courtesy per NASA media guidelines).
- **Dimensions:** 5,173 × 3,449 px (4.16 MB JPEG, progressive). Aspect ratio 3:2 — clean landscape, crops beautifully to 16:9 or 21:9.
- **Why it works:**
  1. Real photographic register — counterbalances Option 1's painted quality if the panel wants the page to feel more "now."
  2. Cassada's frame is dominated by a deep-black space horizon with reds, greens, and purples curling above clouds. The purples already approach brand color.
  3. NASA Earth Observatory has the strongest possible license posture (true PD via federal authorship), so legal review is trivial.
- **Treatment recommendation:** Crop to 21:9 (cinematic banner) using the horizon as the lower third. Apply a `#9000F0` overlay at ~40% opacity, slightly multiply-blended, to unify the green and red wavelengths into the brand violet. Keep a darker right-side gutter for headline copy. Optional: light grain pass to age the image slightly so it reads more "archival" than "Instagram."

## Option 3 — Josef Gabriel Frey, *Ansichten atmosphärischer Phänomene* (1878)

- **Title:** Josef Gabriel Frey, "Ansichten atmosphärischer Phänomene" — gouache chart depicting will-o'-the-wisp, moonbow, northern lights, simoom, water spouts, lightning, and other atmospheric phenomena, painted by Frey in his 88th year, Weyer, Austria, 1878.
- **Direct image URL:** `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a3/Josef_Gabriel_Frey_Ansichten_atmosp%C3%A4hrischer_Ph%C3%A4nomene_1878.jpg`
- **Source page:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Josef_Gabriel_Frey_Ansichten_atmosp%C3%A4hrischer_Ph%C3%A4nomene_1878.jpg (featured by The Public Domain Review: https://publicdomainreview.org/collection/phenomena-over-and-under-the-earth-1878/)
- **License:** Public Domain Worldwide. Underlying work PD (author died 1884; PD-old-100). Digital scan also tagged PD on Wikimedia Commons.
- **Dimensions:** 2,047 × 1,494 px (1.16 MB, JPEG). Aspect ratio ~1.37:1 — landscape, marginally above the 2000px minimum.
- **Why it works:**
  1. The single most on-brief image in the entire search: it is *literally* a 19th-century documentary catalogue of unexplained atmospheric phenomena, painted by hand. The visual metaphor for what Paradocs *is* could not be more exact.
  2. Mood is muted, painterly, slightly mysterious — never gimmicky.
  3. The piece is many small panels in a chart-like layout, which gives the hero image a "specimen sheet" or "archival plate" feel that resonates with the catalogue identity.
- **Treatment recommendation:** This image is busier than the other two and lower-resolution. Use full-bleed at the original 2,047px width capped to a 1,600-tall hero, apply a heavier `#9000F0` overlay at ~65% opacity to flatten the multicolored panels into a unified violet field, and let only the gold/ochre highlights of the lightning, will-o'-the-wisp, and moonbow punch through. Headline anchors to the bottom-left over the darkest sky panel. Risk to flag: at 2,047px wide this is borderline for retina displays larger than ~14"; if the page is intended for desktop hero >1,800px, lean toward Option 1 or 2.
