# "Cases connect. Patterns emerge." Research Hub Card — SME Panel Review

**Date:** May 24, 2026
**Surface:** Research Hub upgrade prompt card (paywall/conversion entry to Core $5.99/mo tier)
**Trigger:** Chase note V11.17.14 — "needs further improvement wrt its design and UI/UX... bring the panel together and also ensure we're aligned with the overall product voice and market targets etc"
**Current copy from screenshot:**
- Eyebrow: FROM PARADOCS · RESEARCH HUB
- Headline: "Cases connect. Patterns emerge."
- Body: "Take any case in Paradocs and trace its connections across the entire archive."
- Three feature pills: Cross-reference / Pattern detection / Build your RADAR
- CTA: Unlock Research Hub
- Footer: Included with Core ($5.99/mo) · Free 7-day trial

---

## What the card has to do (job to be done)

This is the **highest-stakes UX surface in the product** — it's the conversion moment from free user to paid Core subscriber. Specifically: it must do four things in 7 seconds (rough scroll-stop time):

1. **Convince the user they need this** (a need they didn't know they had until this card existed)
2. **Convey what "Research Hub" actually is** (a specific tool, not vapor)
3. **Make the $5.99 feel low** (not by hiding it, but by anchoring it to value delivered)
4. **Remove the trial-friction** (7-day free should reduce risk perception to ~zero)

---

## Panel composition

- **A — Mass-market converter (29M, IT support, suburban Atlanta):** Subscribes to 4 services already (Netflix, Spotify, NYT, ChatGPT Plus). Has $5.99 mental budget room. Skeptical of "Research Hub" framing — sounds academic. Came in via a friend's TikTok.
- **B — Power user / researcher (47F, librarian, Madison WI):** Already loves the product. Wants Research Hub. Will pay anything reasonable. Cares whether the tools are real, not whether the marketing is slick.
- **C — Skeptical first-time visitor (33M, software eng, Berlin):** Found site via Hacker News. Healthy bullshit-detector. Won't pay anything until he's seen substantive content for free. Reading this card on first visit.
- **D — Copywriting director (44F, formerly Apple + Calm + Headspace):** Reviewing voice + conversion craft.

## Persona feedback

### Persona A (Mass-market converter)

> "I want to like this card but I keep bouncing on 'Research Hub.' That word combo doesn't appear in my normal life. I'm here for *paranormal cases*, not *research*. Show me 'find the pattern across all reports' and I'm in. Make me feel like a detective, not like I'm in grad school."

Specific feedback:
- "Research Hub" reads as institutional / academic / something-only-real-PhDs-use
- "Cases connect, patterns emerge" is poetic but not actionable — what does CLICKING do?
- "Build your RADAR" — completely opaque to him. Acronym? Tool name? Personal dashboard?
- The three feature pills should answer "what will I get to DO", not "what abstract capability exists"
- Wants: "Discover what 47,000 cases agree on" type framing — concrete numbers, concrete payoff
- Trial reassurance is good but should be more prominent ("Try free for 7 days" is small footer text — should be inline with CTA)

### Persona B (Power user)

> "I want this. The card is selling me on something I already want — so it's hard for me to grade it. But: 'Cross-reference / Pattern detection / Build your RADAR' is generic. WHAT do I cross-reference? Reports? Cases? Witnesses? What patterns does the AI find that I couldn't find with a search box? Show me one specific finding the product has made — 'the Hessdalen lights correlate with a 27-year geomagnetic cycle' — and I'm sold."

Specific feedback:
- Loves "Patterns emerge" — captures the analytical promise
- Hates the generic feature list — wants ONE specific example of a pattern the product surfaced
- "Build your RADAR" intrigues but needs to be defined (or renamed)
- Pricing feels appropriate; doesn't even need the trial-reassurance — would pay $14.99 directly

### Persona C (Skeptical engineer)

> "Two red flags: (1) the headline rhymes ('connect, emerge') which makes me suspicious of marketing-speak; (2) 'Pattern detection: AI finds emergent patterns across millions of reports' triggers my AI-slop alarm. *What kind of patterns?* If I can't see one before paying, I assume the answer is 'whatever the AI hallucinated this morning.'"

Specific feedback:
- The poetic headline reduces perceived rigor
- Generic "AI finds patterns" claim is meaningless — every product says this. Be specific or don't claim it.
- Wants to see actual research-hub output BEFORE paywall: a "preview pattern" or "example of what you'll find"
- $5.99 is fine but won't pay until trust is established
- Would respond well to "free for 7 days, see real findings, then decide"

### Persona D (Copywriting director)

> "The headline is the best part. 'Cases connect. Patterns emerge.' has rhythm and confidence — Apple-tier copy craft. The body sentence underneath kills it: it's a generic capability statement. The supporting feature pills are a step backward into corporate B2B template-speak."

Specific feedback:
- **Keep** the headline rhythm — that's the asset
- **Rewrite** the body: replace "Take any case in Paradocs and trace its connections across the entire archive" with a *promise of personal capability change*: e.g., "Spot patterns experts missed for decades."
- **Replace** the three abstract feature pills with **one concrete example** + **one capability outcome**:
  - "See how 1,247 ghost sightings cluster by old battlefields"
  - "Trace a 1972 UFO case across 9 categories"
- **Move** the "Free 7-day trial" pill INLINE with the CTA. Trial-friction-removal is the conversion lever — don't hide it in 11pt footer
- **Remove** "FROM PARADOCS" eyebrow — it's the company's own page; redundant
- **Tighten** the "Unlock Research Hub" CTA to "Start Research Hub" or "Open Research Hub" — "Unlock" implies gated content, which is correct, but feels gamified-app rather than tool-grade

---

## Synthesis

The headline is good. The body, feature pills, eyebrow, and CTA verbiage all weaken the offer. The card is **doing too many small jobs and not the one big job**: prove there's a finding worth $5.99/month.

The four personas converge on **show me one real pattern as proof** before the paywall. The current card asks the user to imagine + trust. The high-conversion version *demonstrates*.

The platform also has a positioning misalignment that the card amplifies — "Research" connotes academic gatekeeping; the actual product is closer to an investigative detective's case board (which is broadly appealing). Re-framing as "the detective's tools" rather than "researcher's tools" widens the TAM significantly.

## Recommended treatment

Two versions worth A/B testing:

### Version 1 — "Detective re-frame" (Persona A optimization)

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                              │
│              · CASE TOOLS ·                  │
│                                              │
│         Cases connect. Patterns emerge.      │
│                                              │
│   Spot the patterns experts missed —         │
│   across 70,000+ cases, decades, continents. │
│                                              │
│   ─────  what's inside  ─────                │
│                                              │
│   "1,247 ghost sightings cluster near        │
│    old battlefields"                         │
│                                              │
│   "1972 UFO case traced through 9 unrelated  │
│    categories of paranormal events"          │
│                                              │
│                                              │
│   [ Try free for 7 days  →  ]                │
│   then $5.99/mo                              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Why it works:
- Eyebrow ("CASE TOOLS") drops the academic baggage
- Keeps the strong headline
- Body promises personal capability change
- Two concrete examples of patterns (replace stock features with proof)
- CTA leads with the trial, anchors price below (conversion best practice)

### Version 2 — "Researcher dignity" (Persona B optimization)

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              · RESEARCH HUB ·                │
│                                              │
│         Cases connect. Patterns emerge.      │
│                                              │
│   Cross-reference any case against the       │
│   entire 70,000-case archive — by location,  │
│   date, witness profile, or phenomenon.      │
│                                              │
│   This week's surfaced pattern →             │
│   "47% of southwest abduction reports name   │
│    a Pleiadian description; near-zero in     │
│    other regions."                           │
│                                              │
│                                              │
│   [ Try Research Hub free for 7 days  ]      │
│   $5.99/mo after                             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Why it works:
- Retains "Research Hub" framing for B-persona dignity
- Replaces capability claims with one rotating example finding
- "This week's surfaced pattern" suggests the product is alive — patterns keep emerging
- Engineering implication: need a "featured-finding" generator (similar to Cluster Pattern Card's recommendation in panel review #5)

## Recommendation to Chase

**Ship Version 2** for the next homepage iteration (Research Hub framing keeps brand consistency with the rest of the product). A/B test Version 1 ("Case Tools" re-frame) against it after baseline conversion data exists.

Both versions require an engineering investment: a **Featured Finding generator** that runs weekly on the corpus and outputs one concrete pattern statement per category. This is the shared dependency for both this card AND the Cluster Pattern card.

Build cost: ~1-2 days dev work (Haiku call across phenomenon-clusters, output 1-2 sentences per finding, cached + manually-curatable). Run cost: ~$1/week.
